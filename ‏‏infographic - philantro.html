The Hidden Network: How the Money Flows

A key finding is the role of intermediary "fiscal sponsors" like WESPAC and Tides. They receive funds from multiple foundations and channel them to smaller, often more radical activist groups, creating a complex and opaque funding ecosystem.

A Tale of Two Families

Family dynamics play a dispositive role, offering powerful case studies in philanthropic evolution.

Generational Succession

The Soros Family

In 2023, George Soros passed control of the $25 billion OSF empire to his son, Alex. This transition signals a potential strategic shift for one of the world's most influential philanthropies, raising questions of continuity versus change.

Ideological Schism

The Gelman Family

A striking paradox: The Morningstar Foundation, run by mainstream pro-Israel philanthropists, is a primary funder of their daughter's Bafrayung Fund, which finances radical anti-Israel groups. This highlights a generational split where inherited wealth is repurposed for causes opposing the values of the generation that created it.

A Landscape of Controversy

Each foundation navigates significant criticism, from historical legacies to modern funding choices.

Open Society Foundations

A frequent target of global conspiracy theories, often with antisemitic undertones. Criticized for funding progressive causes and groups involved in anti-Israel campaigns.

Rockefeller Brothers Fund

Confronts the historical legacy of the Rockefeller Foundation's funding for eugenics research, alongside modern criticism for funding groups that lead the BDS movement against Israel.

Elias Foundation

Maintains a lower profile, with controversies being mainly administrative. However, its funding of the WESPAC intermediary links its local work to a larger, more controversial national activist network.

Bafrayung Fund

At the center of controversy for being a key financial backer of radical anti-Israel groups instrumental in recent campus protests, creating a stark contradiction with its own family funding source.
